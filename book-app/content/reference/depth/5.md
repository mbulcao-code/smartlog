# DEPTH DIVE 5 — The Hypothetico-Deductive Method

### Why the Variant Experiment Is the Scientific Method

---

The most successful knowledge-generating practice in human history is not intuition, authority, or tradition. It is a specific structure for turning uncertain beliefs into testable predictions and updating the beliefs based on whether the predictions hold.

That structure is the hypothetico-deductive method. It is also, structurally, exactly what the variant experiment does.

---

**The four steps:**

1. **Observation:** notice something that requires explanation. Price seems to behave differently when entry is taken at the structural level vs. above it.

2. **Hypothesis:** form a specific, falsifiable claim. "Structural-level entries (Variant B) outperform early entries (Variant A) in my primary setup, as measured by win rate and average R:R over 20+ instances."

3. **Prediction:** derive a testable consequence. "Over the next 25 instances of each variant, Variant B will show a higher win rate and average R:R than Variant A."

4. **Test:** observe the actual outcomes and compare to the prediction. The hypothesis is supported, refuted, or refined based on what the data shows.

The critical requirement — the one that makes this scientific rather than pseudo-scientific — is **falsifiability**: the hypothesis must be stated in a way that a specific result could prove it wrong. "Variant B might work better sometimes" is not a hypothesis. "Variant B outperforms Variant A by at least 10 percentage points in win rate across 25 instances" is.

---

**What makes a trading experiment valid:**

A valid variant experiment requires four properties:

**Pre-registration:** the variants must be defined before the data is collected, not after. Defining Variant A and Variant B after you've already logged 20 trades and can see which looks better is not hypothesis testing — it is pattern-fitting to noise. The same data that generated the hypothesis cannot be used to confirm it.

**Consistent criteria:** every instance of each variant must be selected by the same criteria. If Variant A includes "entered before the level" and Variant B includes "entered at the level or below," every logged instance must be cleanly categorisable into one or the other. Mixed or ambiguous instances should be logged as such and excluded from the comparison.

**Sufficient sample:** as detailed in the Probabilities Depth Dive, fewer than 20 instances per variant produces noise-dominated comparisons. The comparison is directional below 20, meaningful at 20–30, reliable above 30.

**Single variable:** the ideal variant test changes one thing at a time. "Variant A vs. Variant B" should differ on exactly one dimension (entry timing, entry trigger type, position size) — not two or three simultaneously. When multiple variables change between variants, it's impossible to attribute the performance difference to any specific one.

---

**Why this matters beyond the method:**

The hypothetico-deductive structure does something psychologically important that no amount of principle-based trading advice achieves: it converts the trader from a subject of the market's judgment into an investigator of a specific question. The market's verdict on any individual trade is no longer a judgment of the trader's competence — it is data for an ongoing investigation.

This reframe is not cosmetic. The Critic's primary argument is "the loss tells you something about you." The investigator's frame converts the same loss to "the loss tells me something about this variant's performance in this condition." The Critic loses its raw material. Not because the trader becomes indifferent to losses — but because the losses have been recruited to a different purpose.

The investigator identity — the trader who is running an experiment, collecting data, and will draw a conclusion from the results — is one of the few identity frames that makes the self-blame loop structurally impossible to run. You can't blame yourself for the results of a properly controlled experiment. The results are evidence. The evidence goes into the analysis. The analysis produces the next hypothesis.

---

**The limiting case — what trading can't test:**

Not everything a trader wants to know is testable by this method. Specifically: questions that require simultaneous live experience of both variants are not directly testable (you cannot hold a position and simultaneously test what would have happened if you hadn't). The counterfactual — "would I have made more if I'd held?" — is answered by tracking what price did after the early exit, not by experiencing both outcomes simultaneously.

For this reason, the log must capture post-exit price behaviour, not just trade outcomes at exit. The comparison between actual exit and what-would-have-happened-if-held is the closest approximation to a controlled experiment the live trading environment allows.

---

**The research:**

Karl Popper's *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (1959) established falsifiability as the demarcation criterion for scientific claims. The hypothetico-deductive structure was formalised in the philosophy of science literature through Hempel and Oppenheim's work on the deductive-nomological model (1948). The specific application to belief updating in contexts of uncertainty draws on Bayesian reasoning — where prior beliefs are updated by likelihood ratios derived from evidence — making each variant comparison a formal update on a prior about which strategy is superior.
